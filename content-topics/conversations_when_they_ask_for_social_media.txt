When They Ask for Social Media
Conversation Script - Phase 2

Sooner or later, most kids ask for social media.
This moment is not just about yes or no.
It is about readiness, identity, belonging, and family trust.

1. Get Curious Before You Decide
Start by asking why they want social media now.
Are they feeling left out, trying to stay connected, or wanting a creative outlet?
The reason matters, because the real need may be connection more than platform access.
Curiosity lowers conflict and helps your child feel respected.

2. Talk About Readiness, Not Just Age
Age matters, but maturity matters more.
Discuss practical readiness: handling pressure, asking for help, and making good decisions when adults are not present.
Ask how they would respond to mean comments, private messages from strangers, or pressure to post something risky.
Their answers show where coaching is needed.

3. Make Risks and Protections Explicit
Walk through privacy settings, public versus private accounts, and what should never be shared.
Explain that posts can spread fast and be hard to take back.
Agree on non-negotiables: no unknown followers, no location sharing, no secret second accounts.
When expectations are explicit, arguments usually decrease.

4. If You Say Yes, Attach Clear Conditions
A yes should include clear structure.
Define platform, time boundaries, account privacy, and review checkpoints.
Set a 30-day trial with a follow-up discussion on what is working and what needs to change.
A conditional yes protects the relationship and teaches accountability.

5. If You Say Not Yet, Give a Path Forward
Not yet works best when it includes a plan.
Explain exactly what skill or behavior you need to see first.
Set a date to revisit the conversation.
This keeps trust intact and shows your child that growth can change the decision.
