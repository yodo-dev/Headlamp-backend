How to Talk About the First Phone
A Headlamp Conversation Guide for Parents

Giving your child their first phone is a major family milestone.
It is not only about a device. It is about trust, responsibility, and the kind of digital habits your family wants to build.
Use this script to start strong before the phone ever arrives.

1. Start Before You Buy the Phone
The best conversation happens before the first setup screen appears.
Begin talking a few weeks early, while emotions are lower and everyone can think clearly.
Ask what your child believes a phone is for, what they are excited about, and what they are nervous about.
Listen first. Advice lands better when kids feel heard.

2. Define the Purpose Together
A phone can be a safety tool, a communication tool, and sometimes an entertainment tool.
Clarify which purpose comes first in your family.
If the purpose is clear, limits feel fair instead of random.
Ask your child to describe the top three things they want to use a phone for.
Use that as the base for your agreement.

3. Make a Family Phone Agreement
Do not rely on vague promises.
Write clear agreements for bedtime, school hours, charging location, app downloads, and respectful communication.
Add what happens if the agreement is broken, and what happens when trust is rebuilt.
Keep the tone collaborative: this is about practicing responsibility, not catching mistakes.

4. Normalize Asking for Help
Tell your child now: if something uncomfortable happens online, they can come to you without losing your respect.
Fear of punishment often keeps kids silent.
Make honesty the safest option.
You can say: if you show me right away, we solve it together.

5. Plan Check-ins, Not Surprise Inspections
A healthy phone culture is built through regular short check-ins.
Set one weekly moment to ask what is going well, what feels hard, and what support they need.
The goal is coaching, not policing.
Small steady conversations build long-term trust.
