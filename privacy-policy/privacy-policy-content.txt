Privacy Policy for Headlamp for Families
Effective Date: January 23, 2026
Headlamp For Families (“Headlamp,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the trust, safety, and personal information of the families who use our mobile application, Headlamp for Families (“the App”).
This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, what we do not collect, how we use information, how long we retain information, and what rights parents, legal guardians, and users have regarding their information.
Headlamp for Families is a reflection and coaching tool designed to help parents and their children have healthier conversations about autonomy, attention, readiness, goals, technology use, and family expectations.
Headlamp is not a monitoring tool.
Headlamp is not a surveillance app.
We do not track children’s location, monitor their messages, scan their browsing history, read their social media accounts, or secretly observe their digital activity. Our purpose is to support trust, reflection, coaching, and better family conversations.
By using the App, you acknowledge and agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.
1. Who This Policy Applies To
This Privacy Policy applies to:
Parents or legal guardians who create and manage a Headlamp account
Minors who use the App under the supervision and approval of a parent or legal guardian
Other authorized family members who participate in a family account
The App is intended for use in a family context. A parent or legal guardian must initiate account registration and approve participation by any minor.
Children under the age of 13 may not use the App unless a parent or legal guardian has provided verifiable parental consent. Consent may be obtained through parent account creation, email verification, and explicit approval within the App before a child participates.
Parents and legal guardians are responsible for deciding whether the App is appropriate for their child and for managing their family’s use of the App.
2. Our Privacy Commitments
Headlamp is built around a simple principle: families should be supported, not surveilled.
We make the following commitments:
We do not sell personal information.
We do not rent personal information.
We do not use personal information for targeted advertising.
We do not collect personal data from a child’s reflection responses.
We do not store child reflection responses in a way that can reasonably identify the child.
We do not use a child’s reflection responses to build behavioral profiles.
We do not use a child’s reflection responses for advertising.
We do not use a child’s reflection responses to train artificial intelligence models.
We do not monitor text messages, emails, calls, web browsing, social media, photos, videos, or location.
We do not secretly monitor children.
We do not provide diagnostic, medical, therapeutic, or mental health services.
Headlamp is designed to help families build trust, clarity, confidence, and better conversations.

5. Child Reflection Responses
Headlamp may invite minors to respond to reflection prompts, readiness questions, goal-setting exercises, coaching activities, or other guided reflection experiences.
We want to be very clear:
Headlamp does not collect personal data from a child’s reflection responses.
Child reflection responses are only stored in a way that cannot reasonably identify the child.
This means:
We do not store a child’s reflection responses with the child’s name, email address, account identity, or direct personal identifiers.
We do not use a child’s reflection responses to identify the child.
We do not sell, rent, or share a child’s reflection responses.
We do not use a child’s reflection responses for targeted advertising.
We do not use a child’s reflection responses to build behavioral advertising profiles.
We do not use a child’s reflection responses to train AI models.
We do not allow Headlamp staff to routinely review individual child reflection responses.
We do not share child reflection responses with schools, employers, advertisers, data brokers, or unrelated third parties.
Depending on how the App is configured, a child’s responses may be processed to generate an in-app experience, such as a reflection summary, family conversation prompt, coaching insight, readiness snapshot, or goal-setting guide. This processing is used only to provide the feature requested by the family.
Headlamp is not designed to use children’s reflections as a record of behavior, discipline, risk scoring, surveillance, diagnosis, or evaluation.
Parents and minors should avoid entering highly sensitive information into reflection prompts, including passwords, financial information, precise location, medical records, therapy records, details about abuse, or emergency information. Headlamp is not a crisis response tool.

The App may also generate family-facing summaries, conversation prompts, coaching insights, or readiness snapshots based on participation in guided activities.

The App should clearly communicate:

What information a minor can see

15. Data Retention
We retain information only for as long as reasonably necessary to provide the App, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, maintain security, and support family accounts.
Our retention practices are as follows:
Parent Account Information
Parent account information, including name, email address, account settings, and authentication information, is retained while the account is active.
If a parent closes the account or requests deletion, parent account information will be deleted or de-identified within 30 days, unless we are legally required or permitted to retain it longer.
Minor Profile Information
Minor profile information, such as first name, nickname, age range, family relationship, and parent-approved settings, is retained while the family account is active and parental consent remains in place.
If a parent revokes consent, removes a minor profile, closes the family account, or requests deletion, minor profile information will be deleted or de-identified within 30 days, unless we are legally required or permitted to retain it longer.
Child Reflection Responses
Child reflection responses are not stored in a way that can reasonably identify the child.
Any child reflection data used to generate an in-app coaching insight, summary, readiness snapshot, or conversation prompt is retained only as long as needed to provide that feature.
Where temporary processing is required, reflection response data is deleted or de-identified within 24 hours after the feature is provided, unless the family chooses to save a resulting non-identifying summary or prompt within the App.
Family-Facing Summaries, Prompts, or Coaching Insights
Family-facing summaries, prompts, readiness snapshots, or coaching insights may be retained in the family account if the parent or family chooses to save them.
Saved family-facing content is retained while the family account is active.
If the family deletes saved content, closes the account, or requests deletion, the content will be deleted or de-identified within 30 days, unless we are legally required or permitted to retain it longer.
Parental Consent Records
Records of parental consent may be retained for up to 3 years after the account is closed or consent is revoked, unless a longer retention period is required by law.
These records are retained only to demonstrate compliance with legal obligations.
Support Communications
Support emails, requests, and related communications may be retained for up to 2 years after the last interaction, unless a longer period is needed to resolve a dispute, comply with law, or protect the safety and security of users.
Technical Logs and Security Records
Technical logs, authentication logs, crash reports, and security records are retained for up to 12 months, unless a longer period is needed to investigate security incidents, prevent fraud or abuse, comply with law, or maintain the integrity of the App.
Analytics Data
Analytics data is retained for up to 24 months.
Where possible, analytics data is aggregated, de-identified, or minimized before retention.
Analytics data is not used for targeted advertising, surveillance, or child profiling.
Legal and Compliance Records
Certain records may be retained for longer when necessary to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, respond to lawful requests, or protect the rights, safety, and security of Headlamp, users, or the public.
Parents may request deletion of their family’s data by contacting:
hello@headlampforfamilies.com
Once verified, we will delete or de-identify the relevant information according to the timelines described above, unless we are legally required or permitted to retain it.
